ATP proof searches are restricted by wall clock timeouts, which depend significantly on a system's speed and state. Changing the timeout from the standard of 3 seconds to 10, 20, or more seconds can be done by inserting \verb+[timelimit X]+ commands. The timeouts in this formalization are chosen such that the proof runs through on a mid-range consumer laptop (ASUS Zenbook 15, Linux). Making the code run on a different laptop may require increasing the timeout or, in very stubborn cases, adding additional proof steps. Sometimes, changing the ATP by inserting \texttt{[prover vampire]}, for example may prove helpful as well.
